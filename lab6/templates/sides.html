{% extends "layout.html" %} {% block image %} {% endblock %} {% block recipename1 %} Moros y Cristianos (Black Beans and Rice) {% endblock %} {% block info %} This recipe is a delicious mix of black beans and rice. The rice absorbs the taste and color of the beans. This dish actually has a deep, cultural history. The dish is said to have originated during the Moor's invasion of Spain, and later brought to Cuba via Spanish invaders. I usually just call the dish black beans and rice, or congris (with grey). {% endblock %} {% block ul1 %}
  • 1lb dried black beans, picked and rinsed well
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 large white onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1T olive oil
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 1cup sofrito
  • 2t salt
  • 1t black pepper
  • 1/2 t dried oregano
  • 1/2 t ground cumin
  • 2 cups extra long white rice
{% endblock %} {% block ol1 %}
  1. Soak the beans overnight with 10 cups of water. Add the next five ingredients to the beans and bring to a simmer over medium heat. Simmer until tender, and skim the foam that rises to the top, 45-60 min. Drain the beans and reserve 3 cups of cooking water.
  2. In the meantime, make the sofrito. Warm some olive oil in a pan and saute the sofrito until fragrant, 3-5 min. Add the salt, pepper, oregano, and cumin and cook an additional 2 min.
  3. Add the sofrito to the pot with the beans and 3 cups of cooking water. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Add 2 cups o extra-long white rice. Simmer and then turn down to the lowest setting. Cook covered untli the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender, 20-30 min. Avoid lifting the lid. Let the rice stand 15 min before serving.
{% endblock %} {% block recipename2 %} Yucca con Mojo (Yucca with Garlic Creole Sauce) {% endblock %} {% block info2 %} This recipe features Yucca, a root that has been the chief root crop of Cuba since the time of the indigenous Taino. This dish is traditionally served along side meat dishes, and is an absolute must at holiday gatherings. It is served with an accompanying creole garlic sauce. {% endblock %} {% block ul2 %}
  • 3lbs of yucca, cut into 2" pieces
  • 6 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1t salt
  • 1/4t ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup sour orange juice (Or equal parts lime and orange juice)
  • 2T fresh oregano leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 large yellow onion, sliced
  • 1/2C olive oil
{% endblock %} {% block ol2 %}
  1. Place yuca in a pot with 1T of salt and water to cover. Bring the yuca to a boil, then cover and reduce to a simmer. Continue simmering for 20-25 minutes, until tender.
  2. Drain the yuca, then slice in half and remove the fibrous core.
  3. In the meantime, prepare the mojo. Mash the garlic, salt, and pepper into a fine paste. Whisk in sour orange juice and oregano.
  4. Place onions in a deep skillet with lid. Pour mojo over onions, and toss to coat.
  5. In a separate saucepan, heat up the olive oil. Place the lid over the pot with the onions, and open it just enough to safely pour the oil into the mojo. It will spurt and bubble, immeditely cover with the lid. Leave covered until the popping subsides, 3-5 minutes.
  6. Pour the mojo over the prepared yucca and enjoy immediately.

  • 2 ripe yellow plantains with black spots
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying

  1. Peel the plantain and cut into 1" slices.
  2. In a frying pan, heat oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the slices of plantains to the oil carefully, and cook each side until golden brown, 2-3 minutes.
  4. Remove from the oil, and place on a paper towel lined plate to remove excess grease.
  5. Serve Immediately
